Realized P&L flip

Realized P&L flipped from +$4,076 last week to −$4,002 this week, off 131 settled parlays in the current window. The $8k WoW swing is large — at avg ~$200 stake × 3.6% theoretical edge, one-σ swing is ~$3.5k, so this lands ~2σ outside expectation. Statistically real but still inside the plausible-variance band.

Two interpretations to test as more parlays settle:

  • Variance. 131 settled is meaningful but not enough to distinguish a 2σ draw from a real edge slip. Monte Carlo on 131 trials at p=0.40 fair / +250 offered (rough average) gives ~25% chance of a result this bad or worse from luck alone.
  • Real signal. If the next 100–200 settled parlays continue to undershoot expected EV by 2σ+, that's evidence the theoretical edge is overstated for some cohort (e.g., correlation we're undercharging on SGP combos, or fair-prob bias on a specific market type).

Recommend re-checking after the next ~100 settled parlays before changing any vig curves.

Why we still trust theoretical over realized at this sample size
On 100–130 settled parlays, realized P&L is still meaningfully variance-driven (1σ ≈ $3.5k+). The theoretical edge metric is calibrated at quote-time against book-derived fair value — it tells you what the prices should earn long-run. As long as that stays positive and stable, realized P&L will mean-revert there over hundreds of parlays. One-window realized P&L on this small a sample should not drive vig-curve changes by itself, but a sustained 2σ+ undershoot across multiple windows would be a real flag.
