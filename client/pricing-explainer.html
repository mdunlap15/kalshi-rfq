Three Questions About Parlay Pricing

Q1 and Q2 share one root cause: pp and ROI% are different units. Q3 walks through fair-value calculation.
pp
Percentage points — the absolute arithmetic gap between two percentages.
%
A relative ratio. 5% → 6% is a +1pp move and a +20% move — same change, different units.
FAIR
My estimate of the bet's true win probability. Computed by averaging the de-vigged price across multiple sharp books (full method in Q3). Not a universal truth — the cleanest signal extractable from the market. The chart's "FAIR" line is this number.

Q1: "Books make the most on long odds, but the chart shows tiny longshot margins. Which is right?"

Both. The chart shows pp, not per-dollar edge. Long odds have small pp gaps but huge per-dollar edge.
Short Answer
A 1pp markup is 17% per-dollar edge at +1900, just 2.4% at +120. Same markup, vastly different economics. The proportional version is where longshot profit lives.

Why does pp grow with fair prob in the first place? Vig is multiplicative: offered = fair × (1 + vig). At fair=5% a 2% vig produces a 0.1pp gap; at fair=40% it produces 0.8pp. Geometry, not policy.

Q2: "Your pp average is ~1.5pp but your ROI is ~3.75%. Are you just lucky?"

No. 1.5pp converts to 3-6% per-dollar edge depending on the prob mix. 3.75% sits inside that band.
Short Answer
Same trick as Q1: edge% = pp ÷ offered_prob. A 1.5pp markup at fair=30% is 4.8% per-dollar edge. Across my fill mix, implied per-dollar edge averages 3-6%. Realized 3.75% is consistent with the pricing — not luck.

Caveat: variance is real. A true 5% edge can produce realized ROI between 0% and 8% over a few hundred fills. Long-term ROI lands somewhere in the 3-6% band; sample size will narrow it.

Q3: "How do you compute fair value?"

Per leg: collect each book's two-sided implied probs → drop high-vig outliers → de-vig each book → average → floor at Pinnacle on favorites. For a parlay, multiply leg fairs (with SGP correlation factors).
StepWhat HappensWhy
1 Collect 2-sided book pairs. Each book's home + away American odds → implied probs. Vig only shows when both sides are quoted.
2 Drop high-vig books. Any book whose two sides sum above 106% is excluded. Pin ~102%, DK/FD ~104%. Books above 110% (Asian feeds, retail outliers) corrupt the average.
3 De-vig each surviving book with fair = imp / (imp_home + imp_away). Standard proportional redistribution. Each book yields a (fair_home, fair_away) pair summing to 1.0.
4 Average the per-book fairs. Simple mean across surviving books. Cross-validates noise; one book alone is too noisy.
5 Floor at Pinnacle on favorites. If consensus fair ≥ 50%, take max(consensus, Pin's de-vigged fair). Pin is sharpest. Prevents drift cheaper than Pin on a favorite.

Home leg fair = 63.6%. Add my own vig on top to produce the offered price.

Multi-Leg Parlay: parlay fair = product of leg fairs.

parlay_fair = leg1.fair × leg2.fair × leg3.fair × ...

Three legs at 63.6% / 50% / 55% compound to 17.5% parlay fair.

Two adjustments before the multiply:

  • SGP correlation. Same-game legs aren't independent. Joint prob is multiplied by 1.15 (positive correlation, e.g. fav + over) or 0.90 (negative, e.g. fav + under).
  • Anomalous-totals backstop. If a totals leg's de-vigged fair drifts >10pp from a simpler book-consensus mean, the leg auto-declines.
TL;DR — pp is additive on probability; ROI is multiplicative on dollars. Divide pp by offered prob to convert. Fair value isn't a model — it's a multi-book de-vigged consensus, with Pinnacle as a floor on favorites.
