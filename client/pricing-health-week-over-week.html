Take

The 24-hour numbers tell a coherent story consistent with the recent vig change:

  • Accepted theoretical edge dropped 1.28pp (5.45% → 4.17%) — exactly what's expected when you cut DEFAULT_VIG and relax VIG_BY_LEG_COUNT.
  • That edge cut produced 2.1× fill rate and 2.4× accepted stake — bettors are accepting more offers because the prices are tighter.
  • Net theoretical $ EV is up week-over-week because volume gain (+140% stake) more than offsets edge cut (−24% per-$):
    Last week: 26 parlays × $4,875 stake × 5.45% edge ≈ $266 expected weekly EV
    This week: 64 parlays × $11,709 × 4.17% ≈ $488 expected weekly EV

    ≈ 1.8× expected $ EV at the post-cut pricing.

On the 1-hour delta: 6 vs 8 accepted is too few to interpret — random sampling noise.

Methodology Correction
A prior version of this comparison reported "realized P&L" and "EV per $ wagered" derived from settled-parlay outcomes. That was the wrong frame for pricing health — realized P&L on 14–25 settled parlays is dominated by variance, not by pricing quality. Long-run realized vs theoretical divergence is interesting (it tells you the model is biased), but a one-week slice doesn't carry that signal.

The metric above is theoretical edge at quote time:
SP_edge_per_$ = 1 − (fair_parlay_prob / bettor_implied_prob)
stake-weighted across all accepted parlays in the window.
TL;DR — edge dropped, volume scaled more than enough to compensate, structural shape healthy. The vig change is doing what it was meant to do.
