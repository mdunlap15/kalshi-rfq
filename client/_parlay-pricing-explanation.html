Why "fair × 1% vig per leg" doesn't reproduce the +2470 offer

Short version: the per-leg arithmetic is right, but two adjustments fire at the parlay level after the fair-prob multiplication, plus the per-leg vig isn't a flat 1% on every leg.

Per-leg "My Odds" — almost right, but Leg 2 isn't 1%

The assumption was My Odds = fair × (1 + 1%) per leg. That holds for legs under 50% fair, but Leg 2 (Boston Celtics, fair −209 / 67.6%) triggers a favorite ramp:

vig = baseVig + 0.075 × (fair − 0.50)
    = 0.01   + 0.075 × 0.176
    ≈ 2.3%

Heavy favorites pay more vig per leg to prevent leaks compounding across multi-leg parlays. Legs 1, 3, 4 are all under 50% so they get the flat 1% NBA base.

The bigger issue: vig is NOT compounded across legs

The pricer applies vig once at the parlay level on the raw fair-parlay-prob — not on each leg separately. So multiplying "My Odds" together to back into a parlay price will never reproduce what we offer.

fairParlay = product of fair leg probs            = 3.70% → +2602

effectiveParlayVig = min(0.20,
    max(per-leg vigs)         // ~2.3% here, driven by Boston ramp
  + longshotAdd                // additive, grows as parlay fair shrinks
  + templateRampAdd            // additive, fires on repeated templates
)

offeredImpliedProb = 1 / (1 + (1/fairParlay − 1) × (1 − effectiveParlayVig))

For the +2470 offer: implied ≈ 3.89%, so the effective parlay vig applied was about 4.7%, not 1%.

Where the extra ~2.4pp beyond the favorite ramp comes from

1. Longshot vig add — for parlay fair below a configured threshold (~25%), an additive vig increment scales as fair → 0. At fairParlay = 3.7% this is firing aggressively and is the dominant contributor. (Added to close a dog-parlay gap vs Pinnacle that was bleeding money before.)

2. Template-exposure ramp — if N identical 4-leg parlays (same teams + markets + lines) have already been confirmed in the rolling 24h window, an additive vig increment is layered on. If this template is popular tonight, that's another ~0.5–1pp.

The closest formula attempted (per-leg × 1.01) is the legacy mode

Multiplying each leg by 1.01 (the +2496 estimate) is the per-leg-compounding mode. That mode was turned off because it produced uncompetitive prices on long parlays. Current parlay-level mode is less vig on a 4-leg parlay than per-leg compounding would be — but the longshot ramp pushes it back up because parlay fair is small.

TL;DR
Vig is applied once at the parlay level on the fair-parlay-prob, not compounded per leg. The effective vig is max(per-leg vig, including a favorite ramp on legs above 50%) + a longshot additive that grows as parlay fair shrinks + a template-repeat surcharge if applicable, capped at 20%.

Two side notes

  • The "My Odds" column on the Lines page is a per-leg standalone number — what we'd quote if it were the only leg of a 1-leg parlay. It's not an input to parlay pricing; fair_prob is. Multiplying My Odds across legs will always be wrong.
  • The +2512 from multiplying My Odds is actually less offered (better for the bettor) than the real +2470 — not being short-changed by hidden math. The real price reflects longshot + template surcharges that make sense given the parlay-level economics.
